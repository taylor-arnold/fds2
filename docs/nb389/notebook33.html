This notebook brings back both Italy tables from the last two notebooks and puts them together. it_city is the table of 388 cities, with city_name, lon, lat, and pop; prov is the GeoJSON of Italy’s 107 provinces, with a province name and a polygon geometry. A row of it_city is one city; a row of prov is one whole province boundary. The two are not linked by any shared key column this time: there is no province name on the city table. What links them is geography: each city sits inside exactly one province, and the point of a spatial join is to recover that relationship from the geometries alone.

Spatial operations only work when both tables are in the same coordinate system, so the first question projects both into EPSG:7791 and saves them back. After that, the join and geometry-building steps get reassigned; the plain lookups and counts should just be printed.

leader in kilometers. Nothing raised an error. The join ran fine and returned
distances, just distances in degrees, which are not a fixed length on the
ground. A degree of longitude is shorter than a degree of latitude at these
latitudes, so an east-west gap like Imperia to Cuneo gets undercounted less
